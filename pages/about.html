Harvest Yields is a free open source piece of software designed to predict future crop yields based on historical weather and harvest data.
A demo dataset is provided for the Waddington area of Lincolnshire, UK. You may also enter your own historical data and the algorithm will predict a personalised yield.

The software was created by Błażej Berezowski, Harry Clayton, Alex Jeffs, Jamie Land, John McGuire, Ethan Stretton and Tommy Wilson at The University of Lincoln UK as part of
their Team Software Engineering module.

The software uses libraries such as KNN to take historical weather and crop data from csv files, trains the algorithm, forecasts the weather and then predicts a reasonable assumption for the yield on each of the next 30 days.

Naturally, the running time of the algorithm depends on the size of the data. Also, the accuracy of the prediction drastically increases depeding on the size of the dataset.
The default dataset provided is 2500 records, however a 400,000 record dataset is also provided. The 2500 will take perhaps 20 minutes to run, the 400,000 will take a number of days (depending on the processing power of your computer).
